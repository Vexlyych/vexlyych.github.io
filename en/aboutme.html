About Me

Hello! I'm Dan Mulkey. Born in Fairbanks, Alaska, currently living in Nichinan, Miyazaki. I'm passionate about health, technology, and their intersection, focusing on increasing human ability and wellbeing.

With doctors on both sides of the family, I've been influenced by magnanimous character from a young age. I've also watched as loved ones struggle with failing health, and perfectly healthy minds are denied freedom of movement by neglect and chronic illness. I champion a preventative, multifactorial approach to health, promoting physical, mental, and spiritual actualization of the individual. As a healthcare professional, the primary affective dimension in patient care is, of course, the physical, through active conditioning, nutrition, and remediation of disease and mutagens. In treating a patient's physical needs, however, I believe it's important to support them in all three for a balanced and proactive recovery.

Growing up during the turn of the millenium, I have fond memories of "Web 1.0". I'm a big fan of decentralized communities, open source, and modular software. Troubleshooting is incredibly fun, and helped me develop an approach to problem-solving that is lateral, analytical, and creative. Recently, I've been exploring 3D modelling and printing in an amateur capacity, and am excited to see printed prostheses, orthoses, and personal health monitors gain popularity and accreditation.

I also jazz drum, if you're interested in that.

If you're looking for a flexible problem solver and collaborator for your project, please get in touch. I'd be happy to help.
